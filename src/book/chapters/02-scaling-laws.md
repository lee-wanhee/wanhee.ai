If general methods win as we add computation, the natural next question is *how much* they win,
and *how predictably*. Scaling laws answer this: across many orders of magnitude, loss falls as a
power law in model size, dataset size, and compute.

$$
L(N) \approx L_\infty + \left( \frac{N_c}{N} \right)^{\alpha_N}
$$

This chapter builds these relationships from first principles and asks what they do — and do not —
promise.
