Wanhee Lee

PhD candidate at Stanford University, advised by Dan Yamins.

I’m a PhD candidate at Stanford University, advised by Dan Yamins. My research focuses on scalable autoregressive transformers for visual perception, controllable generation, and multimodal reasoning over spatiotemporal data. I develop unified models that integrate RGB, depth, optical flow, and camera motion — enabling tasks like novel view synthesis, object manipulation, and geometry extraction.

I also write about research and ideas on the blog. You can reach me by email (icon below), on Twitter/X, or find my code on GitHub.
