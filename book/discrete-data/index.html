Part III · Discrete Data

Autoregressive Language Models

The first modality to fall to scale was discrete: text, as a sequence of tokens. Autoregressive models factorize the joint distribution into a product of conditionals,

pθ(x1:T)=∏t=1Tpθ(xt∣x<t),p_\theta(x_{1:T}) = \prod_{t=1}^{T} p_\theta(x_t \mid x_{<t}), pθ​(x1:T​)=t=1∏T​pθ​(xt​∣x<t​),

and train by maximizing likelihood. This chapter develops why this simple objective, at scale, produces such general capabilities.
